🎯 The STAR Method Explained

The STAR method is the gold standard for answering behavioral interview questions. Every top company uses it.

S — Situation

Set the context. Describe the situation you were in. Keep it brief — 1-2 sentences.

T — Task

What was your responsibility? What challenge were you facing? Be specific about your role.

A — Action

What did you actually do? This is the most important part. Focus on "I" not "we". Use action verbs.

R — Result

What was the outcome? Quantify whenever possible. "Increased revenue by 20%" beats "increased revenue significantly."

💰 Salary Negotiation: The 5-Minute Rule

Never accept the first offer. Studies show that negotiating even briefly increases starting salaries by 7% on average.

Rule 1: Always Counter

Even if the offer is fair, counter by 10-15%. The worst they can say is no.

Rule 2: Use Data, Not Emotion

Bring market data (Glassdoor, Levels.fyi) to the conversation. Facts beat feelings.

Rule 3: Negotiate Total Compensation

Base salary, bonus, equity, benefits, signing bonus, remote work flexibility — everything is on the table.

Rule 4: Get It in Writing

Verbal promises mean nothing. Always get the revised offer in an email or formal letter.

Rule 5: Be Willing to Walk Away

The best negotiation position is having another offer. Use CareerOS to apply to 10+ companies simultaneously.
